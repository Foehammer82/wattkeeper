# Code of Conduct

## TL;DR

Be nice. Be honest. Help build cool stuff. Do not be creepy, cruel, bigoted,
or a jerk. If someone says you crossed a line, take it seriously.

## Our Pledge

Wattkeeper should feel welcoming, practical, and safe to build together.

We want this community to be harassment-free and encouraging for everyone,
regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex
characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience,
education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race,
religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

People should be able to ask questions, make mistakes, learn in public, and
improve the project without being belittled for it.

## Our Standards

What we want:

- Being kind, welcoming, and patient, especially with new contributors
- Addressing problems directly and respectfully
- Giving useful feedback that improves the work instead of scoring points
- Accepting correction gracefully and owning mistakes when they happen
- Leaving room for questions and imperfect first drafts

What is not tolerated:

- Harassment, threats, intimidation, or stalking
- Trolling, pile-ons, or repeated bad-faith arguing
- Insults, slurs, degrading remarks, or personal attacks
- Sexualized language, imagery, or unwanted sexual attention of any kind
- Bigotry or exclusionary behavior aimed at protected classes or personal
  identity
- Doxxing or sharing someone else's private information without explicit
  permission
- Retaliation against someone for reporting a problem or participating in an
  investigation
- Other conduct that a reasonable person would view as abusive, hostile, or
  unprofessional

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Maintainers are responsible for enforcing these expectations and may remove,
edit, or reject comments, commits, code, issues, and other contributions that
do not align with this Code of Conduct.

They will communicate moderation decisions when appropriate.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in project spaces and when someone is officially
representing the project in public.

## Enforcement

If someone crosses the line, report it privately to the repository maintainers
through GitHub. Please do not open a public issue for Code of Conduct reports.

Reports will be reviewed promptly and fairly, with attention to the privacy and
security of the reporter.

## Enforcement Guidelines

Maintainers will use these guidelines when deciding consequences:

### 1. Correction

**Community Impact**: Use of inappropriate language or other behavior deemed
unprofessional or unwelcome in the community.

**Consequence**: A private warning explaining the problem and the expected
change. A public apology may be requested.

### 2. Warning

**Community Impact**: A violation through a single incident or series of
actions.

**Consequence**: A warning with clear consequences for continued behavior,
including a temporary or permanent ban if needed.

### 3. Temporary Ban

**Community Impact**: A serious violation of community standards, including
sustained inappropriate behavior.

**Consequence**: A temporary ban from project interaction for a specified
period. Violating the terms of that ban may lead to a permanent ban.

### 4. Permanent Ban

**Community Impact**: Demonstrating a pattern of violation of community
standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior, harassment of an
individual, or aggression toward or disparagement of classes of individuals.

**Consequence**: A permanent ban from project interaction.
